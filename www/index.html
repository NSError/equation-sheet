Equation-sheet

Every equation sheet sucked, so I made a better one.

  • Download PDF
  • View On GitHub

Are you tired of sucky equation sheets in maths classes? Things that are typeset by a monkey on amphetamines during an earthquake? Are you tired of the lack of pretty diagrams and useful identities? Perhaps the paper it was printed on irritates you?

Well, have I got the answer for you! It's actually trivial to roll your own equation sheet in \(\LaTeX\) - and it's easy to make your own diagrams using post-script directives to the \(\LaTeX\) interpreter. But, indeed, the extreme levels of tweaking necessary to perfect this would be onerous and burdensome! Rather than waste your extremely valuable time, why not start with an intelligent default? Perhaps it's good enough that no tweaking is necessary?

Have I got your attention now?

Well then download the equation sheet. Look it over. You'll like it - especially because with just a handy fork you can customise it to your heart's content.

Erratta

I am well aware that this project hardly qualifies as complete. I'm still waiting to take Math 101B and 101C (the rest of calculus). So integral identities and formulae are conspicuously missing as of now. If you'd like to add them, just make a pull-request. I'm happy to help you with any \(\LaTeX\)-fu you may need to get the job done!

It's also in my list of things to do regarding this project to make a better way of generating the diagrams. I think a system using ERB/Thor would work beautifully, so you could control the diagram detail to a certain extent. The ability to use computed numbers instead of hand-computed decimals will make the diagrams look much more accurate at high resolutions, as well. Not that anyone would really care about that.
